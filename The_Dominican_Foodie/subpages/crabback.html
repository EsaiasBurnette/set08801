CRABBACK RECIPE

Crab back is a traditional Caribbean dish that is particularly popular in the Commonwealth of Dominica. The dish involves removing the top shell from large crab backs and then filling the cavity with a mixture of breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, melted butter, onion, green pepper, red pepper, garlic, parsley, thyme, salt, and black pepper. The filled crab backs are then baked until the filling is golden brown and crispy. The result is a delicious and savory dish that is perfect as an appetizer or a main course. Crab back is often served hot with lemon wedges on the side.

Ingredients

  • 6 large crab backs
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped green pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped red pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375Â°F.
  2. Remove the top shell from each crab back and discard any loose pieces. Clean out the cavity of each crab back with a spoon.
  3. In a bowl, mix together the breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, melted butter, onion, green pepper, red pepper, garlic, parsley, thyme, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Divide the mixture evenly among the crab backs, pressing it down to fill the cavity.
  5. Place the crab backs on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the filling is golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve the crab backs hot with lemon wedges on the side.
