DIVE FEST

Dominica's Dive Fest is an annual event that takes place in July, celebrating the island's stunning underwater world and its unique marine life. The event is organized by the Dominica Watersports Association in partnership with local dive operators and businesses.

During the week-long festival, divers from around the world come together to explore Dominica's pristine reefs, drop-offs, and wrecks, which are home to an incredible variety of marine life, including colorful corals, sponges, and schools of tropical fish. Dominica is also known for its deep-sea diving opportunities, with steep walls and underwater canyons providing a thrilling experience for more advanced divers.

In addition to diving, the festival also includes a range of activities and events for visitors to enjoy, such as beach parties, boat tours, and cultural performances. One of the highlights of Dive Fest is the lionfish hunt, which aims to reduce the number of invasive lionfish in the waters around Dominica. The event is both fun and educational, as divers learn about the impact of invasive species on the local ecosystem.

Another popular activity during Dive Fest is whale watching, as the festival coincides with the annual migration of humpback whales to the waters around Dominica. Visitors can take boat tours to observe these magnificent creatures up close, and learn about their behavior and habitat from expert guides.

Overall, Dominica's Dive Fest is a unique opportunity for divers and nature lovers to experience the island's natural beauty and rich marine heritage. It is a celebration of the ocean's wonders and a reminder of the importance of protecting our fragile underwater ecosystems for future generations.

Here is a list of Cultural Dishes that is common during this season

  1. Fish and Provision - a popular dish made with boiled root vegetables such as yams, sweet potatoes, and cassava, served with steamed fish seasoned with a variety of herbs and spices.

  2. Fish Broth - a flavorful soup made with fresh fish, vegetables, and spices, often served with dumplings or breadfruit.

  3. Saltfish and Bakes - a classic breakfast dish made with salted codfish and fried bread rolls, served with avocado or plantains.

  4. Grilled Fish - fresh fish marinated in a mixture of herbs and spices, grilled to perfection and served with a side of salad or rice.

  5. Fish Cakes - savory patties made with salted codfish, flour, and a blend of herbs and spices, deep-fried until crispy and golden brown

  6. Fish Stew - a hearty stew made with fresh fish, tomatoes, peppers, and onions, flavored with a variety of herbs and spices, served with rice or breadfruit.

  7. Conch Fritters - crispy fritters made with conch meat, flour, and a blend of spices, served as a snack or appetizer.

  8. Fish in Coconut Milk - fresh fish cooked in a rich and creamy coconut milk sauce, flavored with garlic, ginger, and hot peppers, served with rice or breadfruit.

  9. Fish and Chips - a local take on the classic British dish, made with fried fish and crispy fries, often served with a side of tartar sauce or hot sauce.

  10. Fish Chowder - a thick and hearty soup made with fresh fish, vegetables, and spices, simmered until thick and creamy, often served with bread or crackers.
