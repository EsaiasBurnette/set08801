The taste of Dominica at your fingertips

Welcome to our site dedicated to showcasing the tantalizing cuisine of the Commonwealth of Dominica! Nestled in the heart of the Eastern Caribbean, this small island nation boasts a culinary heritage that's as rich and diverse as its cultural tapestry. Our site features an exciting collection of mouth-watering recipes, from delectable dishes to refreshing drinks that are sure to satisfy your appetite and quench your thirst. But we don't just stop there - we delve deeper into the history behind each dish, providing fascinating insights into its origin, significance, and cultural context. Come join us on a journey of discovery and exploration through the vibrant flavors and colorful stories that make up the modern cuisine of Dominica.

RECIPES

Callaloo Soup

Callaloo leaves are the main ingredient in this soup. The leaves are typically cooked down with coconut milk and a variety of spices such as garlic, onion, thyme, and hot peppers. The soup is then typically seasoned with salt and pepper to taste and served with a side of bread or dumplings.

Pineapple Punch

Fresh pineapple is blended with sugar, water, and spices such as cinnamon and nutmeg to make a sweet and refreshing drink. The punch is typically served chilled over ice.

Crab Backs

This dish is made by stuffing crab shells with a mixture of crab meat, breadcrumbs, peppers, onions, and other seasonings. The stuffed shells are then baked until golden brown and served hot.

NATURAL REMEDIES

Soursop Leaf Tea - Immune System Booster

Soursop leaves are commonly used in Dominica to make a tea that is believed to have many health benefits. It is said to boost the immune system, reduce inflammation, and improve digestion.

Fever Grass Tea - Fever and Cold Treatment

Fever grass (lemongrass) is another popular herb used in Dominica to make tea. It is believed to have anti-inflammatory properties and is commonly used to treat fever and colds.

Guava Leaf Tea - Blood Pressure Regulator

Guava leaves are used to make a tea that is believed to have many health benefits. It is said to help lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, and promote healthy skin.

VIDEOS

DOMINICAN STREET FOOD

Street food in the Commonwealth of Dominica is a vibrant and eclectic cuisine that reflects the island's rich natural resources and cultural diversity. Dominica's street food scene is largely based on local produce and seafood, which are transformed into delicious and affordable snacks and dishes.

DOMINICAN TRADITIONAL FOOD

The traditional food in the Commonwealth of Dominica is a rich and flavorful cuisine that reflects the island's history, culture, and natural resources. The cuisine is largely based on fresh, locally sourced ingredients, including seafood, vegetables, and fruits.
