DOMINICAN TRADITIONAL FOOD

The traditional food in the Commonwealth of Dominica is a rich and flavorful cuisine that reflects the island's history, culture, and natural resources. The cuisine is largely based on fresh, locally sourced ingredients, including seafood, vegetables, and fruits. One of the most popular traditional dishes in Dominica is the national dish of the island, called "mountain chicken" which is actually made from frog legs. Other popular dishes include "callaloo" which is a soup made from leafy greens, seafood and coconut milk, "rice and peas" which is a dish of rice, beans and spices, and "stewed chicken" which is chicken cooked in a tomato-based sauce with herbs and spices. The traditional food in Dominica also includes a variety of side dishes, such as "provisions" which are starchy vegetables like yams, cassava, and plantains, as well as a variety of hot sauces made from local peppers. Dominica's traditional food is a delicious and unique cuisine that is a must-try for anyone visiting the island.

The Kalinago Territory on Dominica's east coast is home to the Kalinago people, a culture that has remained relatively unchanged over the centuries. The territory is a land of bush doctors, carved totems, and plant medicine. The visitors' center provides information about the Kalinago, and local craftspeople showcase their skills in basket weaving, jewelry-making, and carving. Daniel, a pioneer of cassava bread-making in Dominica, offers his fresh coconut cassava bread at his roadside shop. Jacko Falls, a waterfall down a slippery flight of stone steps in the jungle, is a beautiful and peaceful spot to wade and relax.
