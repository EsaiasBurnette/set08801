DOMINICAN STREET FOOD

Street food in the Commonwealth of Dominica is a vibrant and eclectic cuisine that reflects the island's rich natural resources and cultural diversity. Dominica's street food scene is largely based on local produce and seafood, which are transformed into delicious and affordable snacks and dishes. Some of the most popular street foods in Dominica include the fried and crispy bakes, which are made from a dough of flour, salt, and water and filled with saltfish or meat, as well as the spicy and tangy chicken or fish patties, which are made from a mixture of ground meat or fish and spices, and then deep-fried until golden brown. Other street foods in Dominica include the plantain chips, grilled corn on the cob, and freshly squeezed juices made from locally grown fruits such as mangoes, passionfruit, and soursop. The street food scene in Dominica is a must-try for food lovers looking to taste the flavors of the island and experience its vibrant and colorful culture.

Video Summary

In the above video vlogger Davidsbeenhere starts the day with a tour of the Roseau market in Dominica's capital. They visit Bolo's Mobile and try a saltfish and herring sandwich, which is served on a hot dog bun and accompanied by pepper sauce. The author also enjoys a passion fruit and sorrel juice. Inside the market, they see various vendors selling clothing, household items, and fresh produce. The author samples delicious crab backs, bush rum, sugarcane, tender coconut, coconut water, cow skin, saltfish & bake, and pommecythere juice. They then visit a coconut vendor with a truck full of coconuts, and the author tries the sweetest coconut water and meat they have ever had. The tour continues to the Dominica Football Association offices, where the author buys an authentic Dominica jersey. Finally, they drive uphill through the jungle to visit Freshwater Lake, where visitors can swim and kayak, and Trafalgar Falls, twin waterfalls in the jungle that take 10 minutes to hike through the rainforest to reach.
